Professional Biography

For nearly twenty years I’ve worked as a professional computer scientist with a focus on Linux systems administration. I’m engaged as a Principal System Administrator with SAIC (Science Applications International Corporation). My teammates and I have become key members of the continuing efforts to develop advanced cloud and on-premises solutions utilizing the latest technologies and integration pipelines.

Historically, my focus has been on infrastructure systems and development of distribution-agnostic configuration management solutions for Linux using Puppet, Ansible, virtualization, containers, the HashiCorp suite, and open-source technologies.

However, the art of systems administration and computer science has rapidly evolved with the introduction of techniques and concepts including DevOps, infrastructure as code, cloud and OS-agnostic development, computing at scale, and containerization. This has lead to a great number of learning and teaching opportunities and in the end good work being accomplished.

A side benefit of working with cutting edge technologies, brilliant people, and diverse toolsets is the ability to reach out to other students and professionals, both mature and developing, to expand and improve the art and science of system administration. I’m proud of involvement with the League of Professional System Administrators (LOPSA), East Tennessee (LOPSA-ETENN) chapter where presentations, discussions, and education on multiple topics including systems and network administration and professional development.

In previous positions I’ve held multiple roles with the U.S. Department of Energy’s (DOE) Oak Ridge National Lab (ORNL). I’ve been a member of the Systems Operations group at the National Center for Computational Sciences (NCCS) home of the Titan and Summit supercomputers and also a part of the Research and Development group supporting the Neutron Sciences Directorate’s research programs at the Spallation Neutron Source and High Flux Isotope Reactor.

While with the University of Tennessee’s National Institute for Computational Sciences (NICS) Systems Operations group I held a management role with the NSF-funded Extreme Science and Engineering Discovery Environment (XSEDE) project. As part of the NICS team I initiated and oversaw the design and redevelopment of a Puppet infrastructure servicing HPC, Infrastructure, Security, and Storage teams. Additionally, I was the lead system administrator on a 1024-core, large-memory, SGI UV1000 supercomputer targeting scientific visualization.
