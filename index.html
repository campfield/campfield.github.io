Professional Biography

For over fifteen years I’ve worked as a professional computer scientist, with a focus on system administration. In 2015 I began working as part of the Systems Operations group at Oak Ridge National Lab’s (ORNL) National Center for Computational Sciences (NCCS), home of the Titan and upcoming Summit supercomputers.

My primary focus is on infrastructure systems and development of distribution-agnostic configuration management solutions for Linux using Puppet, virtualization, containers, Python, Perl, shell scripting, along with various DevOps-style workflows.

With NCCS I’m part of a large group of professionals working on large-scale and high-performance computers (HPC) targeting the scientific computing research. Along with other US Department of Energy (DOE) research laboratories, we are advancing the progress of scientific research and the movement towards Exascale Computing.

A side benefit of working with cutting edge technologies, brilliant people, and diverse software toolsets is the ability to reach out to other professionals, both mature and developing, to expand and improve the art and science of system administration. I’m proud to have a contributing leadership and presenting role with the League of Professional System Administrators’(LOPSA), East Tennessee (LOPSA-ETENN) chapter where we present, discuss, and educate on topics in the field.

I’ve previously worked as part of the Systems Operations group with the University of Tennessee’s National Institute for Computational Sciences (NICS) which included a management role in the NSF-funded Extreme Science and Engineering Discovery Environment (XSEDE) project. While at NICS I initiated and oversaw the design and redevelopment of a Puppet infrastructure servicing HPC, Infrastructure, Security, and Storage teams. Additionally, I was the lead system administrator on a 1024-core, large-memory SGI UV1000 supercomputer targeting scientific visualization.
